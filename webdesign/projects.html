Tebipenem Pivoxil Trial

Study Details

A randomized non-inferiority trial protocol evaluating tebipenem pivoxil as an alternative to ceftriaxone for clinically non-responding children with shigellosis. This research addresses the growing challenge of antibiotic resistance in gram-negative bacteria, particularly Shigella, which causes significant mortality among children worldwide.

Qualitative Study to assess Healthcare Providers’ KAP on the management of SAM

Study Details

Severe acute malnutrition (SAM) remains a major contributor to global child mortality each year. Adherence to guideline-based management protocols can significantly reduce these deaths. Nonetheless, case-fatality rates in resource-constrained facilities continue to be high despite the introduction of standard guidelines. This persistent burden may be attributed to inadequate knowledge and inappropriate management practices among healthcare providers. The qualitative study aimed to assess the knowledge, attitude, and practice of healthcare providers concerning the facility-based management of children with SAM in Bangladesh.

My Role

  • Principal Investigator of the project
  • Implemented the whole study in six districts
  • Conducted In-depth Interviews, Key Informant Interviews, Facility Observation
  • Analyzed, interpreted data and published manuscripts

Associated Publications

Islam MR, Fahim SM, Rasul MG, Raihan MJ, Ali NM, Bulbul MM, Ahmed T. Health care providers’ knowledge, attitude, and practice regarding facility-based management of children with severe acute malnutrition in Bangladesh. Food and nutrition bulletin. 2022 Dec;43(4):465-78.

Fahim SM, Islam MR, Rasul MG, Raihan MJ, Ali NM, Bulbul MM, Ahmed T. A qualitative assessment of facility readiness and barriers to the facility-based management of childhood severe acute malnutrition in the public healthcare settings in Bangladesh. CONSTELLATION Study

Study Details

Undernutrition remains a significant challenge in low-resource settings, with limited effective interventions for improving growth outcomes in vulnerable infants. Building on findings from the SYNERGIE trial, there was a need to evaluate Bifidobacterium supplementation in a larger, multi-country context.

My Role

  • Bangladesh site investigator for the Gates Medical Research Institute funded project
  • Coordinating study implementation in Bangladesh

Study Details

Title: A Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study to evaluate the effect of Bi-26 (strain of Bifidobacterium longum, B. infantis) supplementation versus placebo on weight gain in underweight infants.

Funding: Gates Medical Research Institute

Duration: 2023-2025

Countries: Bangladesh, Pakistan, Tanzania, and Kenya

L-Carnitine Supplementation Study

Study Details

Severe acute malnutrition (SAM) and environmental enteric dysfunction (EED) are highly prevalent among children in resource-limited countries like Bangladesh. L-carnitine may play a role in improving growth and ameliorating EED among nutritionally vulnerable children.
